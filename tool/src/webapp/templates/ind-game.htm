Insights into an Industry: Managing a Virtual Unit Trust

[Note that the rules of the �game� are likely to change as the course becomes better defined and the objectives become clearer. The database design would hopefully be largely independent of such changes in the rules, allowing this to be developed early in the process.]

This �share game� is intended as a component of the project-based course for Commerce students. In the �game� each student is made the fund manager of a virtual unit trust in the industry they are investigating. Alternatively, the students could be made portfolio managers for a basket of share, much like the share games in newspapers. The aim is to encourage a competitive spirit in the class, while learning more about the individual companies that make up the industry. Each student is given a portfolio of shares in companies in a specific industry (and possibly other industries) and an amount of money in a bank account. Their task is to manage the portfolio to improve investment returns. This would run for the semester and be given a small slot in each tutorial. This would be very flexible, allowing other unpredictable activities to be easily accommodated and students to use any free time on trading.

The aim should be that there is as little direct intervention as possible from the tutor. However, tutors would need to be aware of the different issues in the news and ideally should participate. The possible problem with the game is that the rich will get richer and the poor poorer. Ways in which a better balance could be obtained need to be considered (e.g. acknowledge people who performed comparatively well).

Trading Interface

�         A database will be used to manage the portfolios of the students.

�         An interface would be used to allow a student to login, buy and sell shares and view their portfolio. We could use Excel, Access or the Web as the interface. Which interface we use will depend on how it is integrated in other activities and how difficult it will be to make the interface secure.

�         Share prices will be collected from the JSE closing prices and other stock exchanges. (A prototype demonstration can be viewed at: http://www.meg.uct.ac.za/downloads/Web/Save_JSE.asp.) The trading will be very simple and will always be bought and sold at closing prices. No attempt to take into consideration the subtleties of real market would be made.

�         It would be possible to give individual student more money to invest, or fine them, depending on their performance in other tasks in the course. The rules for this would need to be very simple and encourage a keen interest in all aspects of the course.

�         Another element that could be introduced is to have students invest in other students� unit trusts. They must evaluate which are doing well and choose the good one in which to invest.

�         A simple demonstration has been created at: http://www.meg.uct.ac.za/asp/Commerce/commerce.asp. This uses a Web interface and is currently very basic.

To simplify things we could trade shares at the last closing price, rather than the next closing price. If we opt for the more correct next closing price, there will be a delay in receiving the notification of the success or failure of Buy and Sell transactions. This introduces interesting dynamics, but would require students to work more regularly at the game than once a week.

Decision-Making Process

The trading must not be allowed to become arbitrary or random (although some people may be lucky with this method). Other parts of the course encourage students to collect company and industry information and organise this on a Web site. The concepts that need to be understood include:

�         Earnings per share

�         Price/Earnings ratio

�         FCF or Free Cash flow

�         EBITDA ??

�         Price

We could expect students to compile this information for their �focus company� and publish this on the Industry Web site. Once this is done, students would be expected to justify each trade they make by providing a reason based on one or more of these factors.
